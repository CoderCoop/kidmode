Why it exists
Your kid wants the phone. You'd love to say yes — but one tap could send an email, delete an app, or buy something.
  • ✕A tap in the wrong place fires off a half-written message or a phone call.
  • ✕Apps get deleted, settings get changed, things get bought — all by accident.
  • ✕So the phone stays out of reach, and playtime never happens. Kid Mode changes that.
How it works

Four things working together so "yes" is always safe.

Playtime stays fun and wide open. The rest of your phone stays exactly as you left it.

📱

One app, full screen

Kid Mode fills the whole screen and stays put. The home button, notifications and app switcher step aside, so every tap lands on the toy — never your inbox or your apps.

An easy way out — for you

To get back to the real phone, hold two fingers on the screen for a second, then tap your PIN. Quick for a grown-up, even one-handed and distracted — but past what little hands can do. Forgot the PIN? There's a hold-to-exit fallback, so you're never locked out.

🌊

Mash-proof play

Ten fingers at once, as fast as they can go. Every touch just makes more light and colour. It never lags, never crashes, and never runs out of things to do.

How you use it

You're at the table. The food hasn't come. Small hands reach for your phone.

Start to finish, here's exactly how it goes — a few easy moves, and nothing broken. (The phones below are just illustrations; the live one to play with is up top.)

STEP 01 · START

Open the app — it locks to one screen.

Tap Kid Mode like any other app. It fills the whole screen and stays there: the home button, notifications and app switcher stop responding, so there's nothing to accidentally leave to. No setup, no toggles — just open it and hand it over.

Locked to one app
STEP 02 · PLAY

Hand it over. Let them play.

Every touch makes light, colour and sound — ripples, popping shapes, a sound pad, comets, sparkles. Ten fingers mashing at once only makes more. Nothing lags, nothing runs out, and there's nothing else on screen to wander into.

STEP 03 · SAFE

Nothing they tap can go wrong.

No email gets sent. No app gets deleted. No call goes out. Every tap lands on the toy and nowhere else — the rest of your phone simply isn't reachable while they play.

Nothing to break
the toy is all there is
STEP 04 · EXIT

Done? Hold two fingers, tap your PIN.

Press and hold two fingers anywhere for about a second — a little "keep holding" bar confirms it's working — then enter your PIN on a pad that reshuffles every time. It's quick even one-handed and distracted. Forgot the PIN? Press and hold the exit button instead. Either way, your phone comes right back.

Under the hood

For the curious: how it actually works.

You don't need any of this to use Kid Mode — but if you build things, here's what's behind the four pillars.

Real device lockdown

On Android the app calls startLockTask(); provisioned as a Device Owner it becomes a true no-escape kiosk with notifications suppressed. On iOS it drives Guided Access / Autonomous Single App Mode on supervised devices.

As a second line of defence, JavaScript traps the hardware Back button and keeps the app in immersive, full-screen mode.

A canvas that can't be overwhelmed

Every touch feeds a fixed pool of pre-allocated animated objects. A burst of taps becomes a burst of cheap shared-value writes on the UI thread (via Reanimated worklets) — never React re-renders or garbage-collection churn — so throughput stays bounded and lag-free.

The parental gate

A two-finger press-and-hold opens the gate; a PIN pad whose digits reshuffle each time defeats positional memory. Each barrier targets something a 0–5-year-old can't yet do — sustained multi-touch, and reading digits.

The "Forgot PIN?" fallback is a timed press-and-hold that needs no stored secret, so it can't be forgotten. In production, the device's own screen-pin / Guided-Access exit (protected by the phone's passcode) is always the ultimate way out.

The stack

React Native + TypeScript, with hand-written native modules in Kotlin and Swift for both platforms. Fully open source under the MIT license, with CI that builds an installable Android APK and iOS app on every change.
