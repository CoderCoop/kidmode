Why it exists
Hand a toddler a phone and within seconds they've dialled a contact, deleted an app, or bought something.
  • ✕Home button, notifications and the app switcher are one stray tap away from chaos.
  • ✕A five-second video turns into a purchase, a phone call, or a factory reset.
  • ✕"Just watch them" is not a security model when small hands move that fast.
How it holds

Four systems, each targeting a skill a toddler lacks.

Every barrier is deliberate. The fun is resilient; the way out is not.

🔒

Device lockdown

Native bridges call Android startLockTask and iOS Guided Access to pin the app. A hardware-Back trap and immersive mode are the second line of defence.

🚪

Parental gate

A multi-finger long-press opens the gate, then four timed corner taps, then a randomized PIN pad. Each step needs a skill a small child doesn't have yet.

🌊

Resilient canvas

Every pointer feeds a fixed pool of animated objects. Mashing becomes cheap shared-value writes on the UI thread — never React re-renders or GC churn.

🧩

Modular activities

A registry of pluggable play modules. Add one file and one entry — the shell adapts on its own. Ships with ripples, pop, sounds, comet, sparkle.

A day it saves

You're at the table. The food hasn't come. Small hands reach for your phone.

Here is exactly what happens next — and why you get to decide when it ends.

STEP 01

You hand it over — and it locks itself down.

The moment Kid Mode opens, the device pins to a single app. The home gesture, notifications and app switcher stop responding. There is nothing to exit to.

startLockTask() · Guided Access · immersive mode
Screen pinned · OS hidden
STEP 02

They play — as hard as they possibly can.

Ten fingers at once, mashing the glass. Each touch blooms a ripple, pops a shape, flings a comet or lights a sparkle. It never stutters and it never runs out. Tap the chips to switch worlds.

A fixed animated pool → bounded, lag-free throughput
STEP 03

They try to get out. They can't.

The Back button is caught and ignored. Swiping up does nothing. The system UI never appears. What a grown-up reads as "trapped", a toddler reads as "the toy just keeps playing".

onBackPress → true · no system chrome, ever
Back trapped
home gesture → nothing happens
STEP 04

You decide it's over — and only you can.

Hold four fingers to wake the gate. Tap the corners clockwise. Then enter your PIN on a pad whose numbers are shuffled every time, so there's no muscle-memory to mimic. The device unlocks the instant you're done.
